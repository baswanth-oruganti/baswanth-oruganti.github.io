About Me

I am a researcher in Quantum Computing and Quantum Chemistry focusing on molecular design, optimization, topology, and quantum computing algorithms. My work aims to bridge topology, chemistry and quantum computing through conceptual and computational frameworks.

Research Vision: UEIT–TOPCT

The Unitary Evolution Inaccessibility Theorem (UEIT) framework investigates fundamental constraints on quantum evolution in Hilbert space. By combining symmetry, topology, and group theory arguments, it seeks to identify intrinsic limitations on state accessibility under unitary transformations.

The broader concept of Topological Obstruction extends these ideas toward a unified description of quantum dynamics, chemical reactivity, and optimization landscapes through a mathematical formulation called Topological Obstruction of Phase Continuity Theorem (TOPCT). This framework identifies symmetry-induced topological barriers leading to symmetry-preserving and symmetry-breaking Lie subalgebras. This framework will be extended to other physical, chemical and computational constraints beyond symmetry in P6V30 research program over the next few years.

Current work focuses on rigorous mathematical foundations and computational realization. Future directions include extensions to quantum algorithms, optimization theory, and small-scale validation on emerging quantum computing hardware platforms.
